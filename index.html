Project Description

The goal of our project is to visualize the liberal or conservative decisions of the United States Supreme Court, and each individual Supreme Court Justice's voting preference. We examine how this changes over time - in different years, under different Chief Justices, and/or when justices are the swing vote - as well as how it differs in various issue areas. Three different visualizations tackle this goal, using data from every justice's vote in every supreme court decision from 1946-2015.

The Data

We use the Supreme Court Database from Washington University in St. Louis in our project. Specifically, we are using the justice-centered and case-centered datasets, organized by issue area/legal provision. The justice-centered dataset includes lines of data about every individual justice's vote in each case, while the case-centered dataset only includes one line of data per case. Attributes relevant to our project include the year, the justice, the chief justice at the time, the direction of the decision, the direction of a justice's vote, the issue area, and the median voter status of justices. In addition, our dataset is extended by Maron Sorenson, Professor of Government & Legal Studies, to include information about the median justice (swing vote) in each decision.
